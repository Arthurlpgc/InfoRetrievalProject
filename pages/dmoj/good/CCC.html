Problem type
Simple Math

Canadian Computing Competition: 2011 Stage 1, Junior #3

In a sumac sequence, t 1 , t 2 , … , t m t_1, t_2, \dots, t_m , each term is an integer greater than or equal 0 0 . Also, each term, starting with the third, is the difference of the preceding two terms (that is, t n + 2 = t n − t n + 1 t_{n+2} = t_n - t_{n+1} for n ≥ 1 n \ge 1 ). The sequence terminates at t m t_m if t m − 1 < t m t_{m-1} < t_m .

For example, if we have 120 120 and 71 71 , then the sumac sequence generated is as follows:

120 , 71 , 49 , 22 , 27. \displaystyle 120, 71, 49, 22, 27.

This is a sumac sequence of length 5 5 .

Input Specification

The input will be two positive numbers t 1 t_1 and t 2 t_2 , with 0 < t 2 < t 1 < 10 000 0 < t_2 < t_1 < 10\,000 .

Output Specification

The output will be the length of the sumac sequence given by the starting numbers t 1 t_1 and t 2 t_2 .

Sample Input

120
71

Output for Sample Input

5
